Espresso Recipes

The Espresso, one of the OG coffee drinks

The espresso was then invented in Venice, Italy back in the early 20th century. The drink was created by businessman Luigi Bezzera who was experimenting with coffee to see how he could make his brew faster.

And now it become the OG coffee drink, you can drink it as it is or creating other coffee recipes with it, which I'll cover it next recipes, and espresso is the main ingridients


Tools you need

  • Grinder (Electric grinder or hand grinder), to grind your fresh coffee beans
  • Espresso maker (The best result is using espresso machine, but you can also use manual espresso maker such as Rokpresso, Flair, Moka pot, or even Aero press), to brew your freshly grind coffee beans
  • Tamper, to compress your coffee ground
  • Scale, to track your dose and time so can repeat making espresso that suit your taste
  • Kettle, this is optional if you using manual espresso maker such as Rokpresso, flair, or Aero press
  • Cup, to catch delicious goowy and sweet once you brew coffee into espresso

Ingridients you need

  • Fresh coffee Beans, idealy ages between 5 to maximum 30 days after being roasted, you can find when it being roasted in the coffee bag
  • Water, to ecstrac all the goodies from your freshly grinded coffee beans

How to brew espresso with proper Espresso Machine

First, we're going through process of making espresso with proper Espresso Machine, don't worry, I'll cover up the process with manual espresso maker later on.

So lets started.

  1. Weight your coffee beans, starts from 16gr up to 22gr of coffee beans, depending on your basket sizes
  2. Set your grinder adjustment to fine adjustment (Tips: set your grinder not to the finest adjustment, but a couple steps away from the finest)
  3. Put your coffee beans to the grinder, and also your portafilter into the grinder spout, and start grinding
  4. Once you finish grinding your coffee beans to the portafilter, start distribude your coffee ground evenly through out your basket
  5. After finish distribude your coffee ground evenly, tamp it, it dosen't matter how force you put, what matter is tamp it evenly flat
  6. Put your portafilter into the espresso machine
  7. Put your scale underneath it to track your yield
  8. Put your cup to catch the yield
  9. Zero your scale, and start brewing by pressing brew button in your espresso machine while simultaniously pressing the timer to track your brewing time, try to aiming 1:2 - 1:2.5 ratios, meaning 1 part of coffee ground to 2 part of water(18gr of coffee to 36gr of yield)
  10. After you nailed your brew ratios, see how much time it take to brew your coffee, if it too fast like below 20 seconds, adjust your grinder finner. If it slow like more than 30 seconds, adjust your grinder coarser

How to brew espresso with manual espresso maker.

Now its time to brew espresso with manual espresso maker, such as Rokpressoor and flair.

Follow the first until the fifth steps above on How to brew espresso with proper Espresso Machine, but usually manual espresso maker use less coffee than espresso machine does, typicly around 12gr to 16gr of coffee ground

The best practice is to weight your your coffee ground while you put it into the basket

  1. Once you've done doing the "Puck Prep", put everything back together to start the brewing process
  2. Turn on your kettle until your water starts boiling
  3. Pour the boiling water into the specific chamber for hot water in your manual espresso maker
  4. And same as the brewing process above, put your scale underneath it and your cup to catch the yield
  5. Start your timer, and start the brewing process by pressing down the mekanism in your espresso maker
  6. The ratios 1:2 - 1:2.5 are still the same as above
  7. After you nailed your brew ratios, see how much time it take to brew your coffee, if it too fast like below 20 seconds, adjust your grinder finner. If it slow like more than 30 seconds, adjust your grinder coarser

How to Tweek Your Espresso

My best advice is always track your dose, time, and yield. So you have much more information and you can repeat making good espresso once you find a perfect recipes that suit you

Try to adjust your grinder finner if you brew your espresso too fast with the ratios. If its too slow, try to adjust your grinder coarser. Try to take time on "Puck Prep" to make it as even as possible will also help your extraction.

Once you find recipes that suit you, and time to complet your extraction is perfect, you'll be rewarding by a good fresh espresso that you can repeat and explore another espresso base recipes in the next recipes articel. Stay Caffeinated, Fellas!
